Should Not Hurry for the Funeral of a Dead Person

“Normally the consciousness of the departed ought to feel no pain for what happens to the body after his or her departure.But there is in the material body itself a consciousness called the “spirit of the form” which takes some time to get completely out of the aggregated cells; its departure is the starting point of a general decomposition, and before its departure it may have a kind of feeling of what happens to the body. That is why it is always better not to be in a hurry for the funeral.” 13 November 1966 (121-22)
