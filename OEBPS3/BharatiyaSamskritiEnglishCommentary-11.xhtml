III. The Life-Value of Indian Culture – the Supreme Achievements of Indian Culture in Its Dealings with Life

“There are three powers that we must grasp in order to judge the life-value of a culture. There is, first, the power of its original conception of life; there is, next, the power of the forms, types and rhythms it has given to life; there is, last, the inspiration, the vigour, the force of vital execution of its motives manifested in the actual lives of men and of the community that flourished under its influence.”24
